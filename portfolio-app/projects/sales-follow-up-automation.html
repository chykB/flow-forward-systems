What The Project Does

The Sales Follow-Up Automation System helps a business capture lead information, track lead stage, generate follow-up steps, and reduce the chance that warm leads are forgotten.

In the current V1 prototype, the project is represented by a static project page and a working rule-based follow-up generator in the browser.

Business Problem

Small businesses often lose leads because follow-up is manual, inconsistent, or forgotten. A lead may come from a website form, social media message, referral, or email, but without a simple tracking process, the next step depends on memory.

This creates revenue leakage through delayed replies, missed follow-ups, unclear lead status, and poor visibility into which inquiries need attention.

Manual Workflow

Current State

A lead sends an inquiry, someone reads it manually, decides what to reply, and tries to remember when to follow up. Lead status may live in email, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, or memory.

Common Failure Points

Follow-ups are delayed, lead quality is unclear, proposals are not tracked, and warm leads may go cold because there is no reminder or owner for the next action.

Automated Workflow

Trigger

A new lead is captured from a form, message, or manual entry.

Workflow Steps

The lead is added to a tracker, assigned a status, given a next follow-up date, and connected to a simple follow-up sequence. The system can later use AI to summarize the inquiry, suggest priority, and draft a response for review.

Final Outcome

The business has clearer lead visibility, fewer forgotten conversations, and a repeatable follow-up process.

Current V1 Functionality

Rule-Based Follow-Up Generator

The current tool asks for business type, offer, lead stage, and tone. It generates a simple follow-up sequence, reminder schedule, human review point, and suggested next action.

Static Project Page

This page explains the business problem, workflow design, AI role, human review point, value estimate, showcase status, and future production path.

Future Production Functionality

Lead Capture

A form captures name, email, business type, offer interest, lead source, and current challenge.

Lead Tracker

Each lead receives a status such as new, reviewed, qualified, needs follow-up, call booked, proposal sent, won, lost, or nurture.

AI-Assisted Follow-Up

AI summarizes the inquiry, suggests lead priority, and drafts a follow-up message for human review.

Automation And Audit Log

Future workflow events can be logged so the business can see when a lead was captured, reviewed, followed up, and moved to the next stage.

Tools And Tech Stack

Current V1 Stack

HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The current prototype runs fully in the browser.

Future Stack

Next.js and TypeScript for the frontend, Python and FastAPI for backend workflow logic, PostgreSQL or DynamoDB for lead data, and AWS services for deployment and monitoring.

AI Role

AI can summarize lead details, classify lead quality, suggest priority, and draft follow-up messages. In V1, this is represented with rule-based browser output. In later versions, AI would run server-side with validation, rate limits, and human review.

Human Review Point

A human should review messages before sending, especially for high-value leads, sensitive requests, custom pricing, complaints, refunds, or proposals that affect trust and revenue.

Business Value And Money-Saving Impact

Revenue Protection

Reduces lost leads by making follow-up visible, scheduled, and repeatable.

Conversion Support

Improves conversion from existing inquiries by reducing delayed responses and forgotten conversations.

Time Savings

Reduces manual tracking and repeated message drafting for common lead follow-up scenarios.

Operational Visibility

Makes it easier to see which leads are new, waiting, followed up, proposal-sent, won, lost, or in nurture.

Reliability And Security Considerations

V1 Constraints

This page is a static demo and does not store lead data. No backend, database, AI API, or authentication is used in V1.

Future Reliability

A production version should validate lead inputs, avoid duplicate lead records, log follow-up events, and handle failed notifications safely.

Data Protection

The system should collect only necessary lead data and avoid storing sensitive information in public frontend code.
