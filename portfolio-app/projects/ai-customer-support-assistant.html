What The Project Does

The AI Customer Support Assistant helps teams sort incoming customer messages, identify urgency, prepare draft responses, and keep sensitive cases under human review.

The first planned demo will use rule-based logic to classify messages before any AI API is added.

Business Problem

Customer messages are often slow to sort, prioritize, and answer. Support requests may arrive through email, forms, chat, social media, or messaging apps, and teams can lose time deciding what is urgent and who should respond.

This creates delayed replies, inconsistent service quality, missed complaints, and unnecessary pressure on support staff.

Manual Workflow

Current State

A customer sends a message, a team member reads it manually, decides the issue type, judges urgency, drafts a response, and decides whether to escalate.

Common Failure Points

Urgent messages may be missed, complaints may not be escalated quickly, repetitive questions consume time, and response quality may vary across team members.

Automated Workflow

Trigger

A new customer message is submitted through a form, inbox, support channel, or manual entry.

Workflow Steps

The message is classified by issue type, scored for urgency and sentiment, routed to the right response path, and prepared with a draft reply when appropriate.

Final Outcome

The support team can respond faster, prioritize risky messages, and keep a record of customer issues and response decisions.

Current V1.2 Functionality

Project Specification

This page defines the support workflow, business problem, AI role, human review points, and future demo direction.

Planned Rule-Based Demo

The first working prototype should classify customer messages with simple rules, such as refund, complaint, technical issue, billing, general question, or urgent escalation.

Future Production Functionality

Message Intake

Collect support messages from forms, email, chat, or CRM/support platforms.

Issue Classification

Classify messages by category, urgency, sentiment, customer type, and required next action.

AI Draft Replies

Generate suggested replies for human review, especially for repetitive support questions.

Response Log

Track message status, assigned owner, response draft, approval state, and final outcome.

Tools And Tech Stack

Current V1.2 Stack

Static HTML and CSS for the project page. The first standalone demo can use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with rule-based logic.

Future Stack

Next.js and TypeScript for the frontend, Python and FastAPI for backend workflow logic, AI provider integration, database storage for support logs, and AWS deployment.

AI Role

AI can classify issue type, detect urgency, detect sentiment, summarize customer context, and draft replies. AI should not send sensitive customer-facing responses without human review.

Human Review Point

A human should review refunds, legal issues, angry customers, complaints, sensitive requests, billing disputes, account changes, and any response that could affect customer trust.

Business Value And Money-Saving Impact

Workload Reduction

Reduces manual support workload by helping teams classify and prioritize messages faster.

Hiring Pressure

Helps avoid hiring pressure for repetitive support tasks by reducing the time spent sorting and drafting common replies.

Response Speed

Improves response time by making urgent issues easier to identify and route.

Customer Trust

Keeps human review in sensitive cases so automation supports service quality instead of weakening it.

Reliability And Security Considerations

V1.2 Constraints

This page is a static project specification and does not process or store customer messages.

Future Reliability

A production version should validate inputs, avoid duplicate tickets, track message status, log approvals, and preserve a clear escalation path.

Data Protection

Support workflows can contain sensitive customer data, so future versions should avoid unnecessary data collection and protect stored messages.
